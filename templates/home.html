Preparation goes a long way

I love hiking, and it brings me immense joy that the great outdoors has never been more accessible. As technology progresses, we inherit amazing tools and information that provides us with the security of knowledge and the confidence to tackle any challenge. I find that there are ample resources for the discovery of hiking destinations wherever you may visit. Any avid traveller can find joy at the mere prospect of planning their escape to some of the most beautiful places in the world.

I value preparation as much as anyone, but like branches on a trail, plans can can snap given enough stress. Mistakes are to be made, and unfortunately for hikers, the solutions might not be so easy to research. The information may available, but what if you can't access it? I find myself with a good sense of direction on the trail, but put me on the road and I immediately turn to the robotic instruction of mapping software from my phone. Alas, these middle-of-nowhere destinations are notorious for being poor providers of cell reception. In these situations, you realize that you really wish you had some sort of direction that'll lead the way home.

Have Directions On Hand

Negligence to prepare for the journey back home has led to more wrong turns than I care to admit. I find that even I, if given just a few context clues, can gather my bearings and get myself home. I created TrekAssure for precisely this reason. Once a trail is chosen, TrekAssure can be used to secure a set of static directions back home, and additional directions to few key locations (e.g. gas station) nearest to the trail. These directions are neatly packed in an "After-The-Hike" pamphlet that you can either download as a text file or email to yourself i.e. files that can be accessed without a connection. Adding TrekAssure to your preparation routine before a hike and securing a pamphlet can save you loads of stress for when you are at your most tired.

If at the end of your hike, you find yourself with cell reception, that's AWESOME! Use it! Get home quickly and safely! However, for those times when you are deep in the mountains and you have no idea where you are, you'll be glad to have a pamphlet to at least get you going in the right direction. Like jumper cables, you'll spend most of the time not needing TrekAssure... until you do. What's 5 minutes to avoid the pain?

TrekAssure is powered by MapQuest's Developer API and all trail information is acquired from the excellent Hiking Project API. TrekAssure is designed for the continental US. Results for international users may not be optimal. This is a project created by Nathan Bryant (LinkedIn).
